One call writes the card 1 opus call k+2 plays × 2 takes

The whole card is written in a single context — so the author sees its own slate while writing it and makes the plays formally different, the way a person naturally would. (The previous architecture ran five parallel "engines" that couldn't see each other; each converged to its own pet construction and whole species of your references were structurally unreachable. Measured, replaced 2026-07-15.)

Take competition 1 small sonnet call

Each play arrived as two takes. A judge reads both aloud and keeps the one that lands on the first pass — "the last five words usually decide." Competition beats prescriptions: nothing is rewritten, the better take just wins.

The guards subtractive only — they can drop, never rewrite

Four screens between the author and the card. Copies are the guards' job so the prompts never need anti-reference pressure:

MORPH / COPY

word-set containment ≥ .62 against the corpus + the entire generation history + every killed caption. The guard forgets nothing.

SIBLINGS

two slate-mates telling the same joke — the later one drops.

RE-SKIN

an identity screen for same-joke-new-nouns (hyenas→raccoons class).

REFINE

trims trailing words that land after the payoff. Removes only — never adds, never rewrites.

Survivors become THE OPTIONS on the reel card — all of them ship to you; clicking one re-renders the reel with it (that click is the strongest taste signal the system can receive).

The chooser picks the default 1 sonnet call · eval-gated

Options are shuffled (position carries zero information), then judged as a reader: pick the hardest hitter — with two vetoes, applied first:

VETO 1 · NOT HIM

soft / self-pitying / couldn't sit in the feed — checked against a live sample of 12 real references (the seam test). References judge the output here.

VETO 2 · TONIGHT'S RERUN

same play as a post already up tonight (the last 10 defaults ride along in the call) — take the next-hardest hitter instead.

Every change to this layer must replay the selection benchmark without regressing (your "should have picked X" corrections are the ground truth). The pick is logged to the feed memory the moment it's made.

The reel gets built downstream of captions

Caption-first: audio matched to the caption's vibe → clips matched to the caption (fit + freshness, visually-distinct picks) → beat-cut → composite. This tab focuses on captions; the clip pipeline has its own diagnostics (/api/debug/clip-sim, clip-probe).

THE LOOP — how your grades drive everything

you grade reels /10 + notes→ learn mines every note
"X would have been an 8" · your rewrites · off-voice calls→ ≥8s + endorsed lines PROMOTED into the corpus
the wall & the bar grow→ THE SENSE re-synthesized from your notes
by the agent, never mechanically→ next night's author knows more

Grades are the only quality signal the system trusts — no internal judge ever decides what's "good." A low grade never deletes a format: a miss is evidence about an execution, not a verdict on a play.

The one place references never go: seeding individual slots. Shown to a slot, a reference gets rewritten — proven four times under four names (anchors, quoted winners, format assignments, sparks). Relevance is enforced at the output instead.
